Arancini

Description

Arancini are a popular Italian street food made of bite-sized breaded and lightly fried spheres of risotto.

Arancini take some effort to prepare but they are truly a delight to eat. This arancini recipe is perfect for snacking or serving as an appetizer with a simple Marinara Sauce. Or, they could easily make a whole meal when paired with a Lemon Parmesan Lettuce Salad or your favorite protein.

Ingredients

  • Arbori rice
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Tomato paste
  • Parsley
  • Butter
  • Chicken broth
  • Parmesan
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • All-purpose flour
  • Whisked eggs
  • Plain panko breadcrumbs
  • oil

Steps

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add 2 crushed, peeled garlic cloves and 1 finely chopped yellow onion.
  3. Stir until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice and toast until semi-translucent and fragrant.
  5. Stir in 1 teaspoon salt, 1/8 teaspoon black pepper, 3 tablespoons chopped parsley leaves, 3 cups chicken broth, and 2 teaspoons tomato paste.
  6. dd 2 tablespoons of butter and bring to a soft boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let simmer for 20-25 minutes.
  7. When the rice is tender and the broth is fully absorbed, turn off the heat and mix in 1/2 cup of grated parmesan.
  8. Carefully spread the rice onto a parchment-lined baking tray and let cool for 10 minutes.
  9. When the rice is cool enough to handle, roll it into about 20 1-inch balls.
  10. Cut the 4 ounces of mozzarella into 20, 1/2-inch cubes. Stuff them into the center of each rice ball, closing them by gently pushing rice over the opening and reshaping the balls.
  11. In one medium mixing bowl, stir together 1/2 cup flour, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1/8 teaspoon black pepper.
  12. In a second bowl, whisk 2 eggs. Add 2 cups of breadcrumbs to a third bowl.
  13. Dredge each ball in flour, then egg, then panko, and place on a clean parchment-lined baking sheet.
  14. Heat 2 cups grapeseed or vegetable oil in an 8-inch saucepan to 350Â° F, or until the oil is bubbling but is not smoking.
  15. Fry 6 balls at a time, gently moving them around until golden in color, about 3-4 minutes. Place fried arancini on a paper towel-lined plate.
  16. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving with toothpicks or napkins on the side.
